Nepal

Nepal is a small, landlocked country located in South Asia, between India and China.
Despite its small size, it is rich in natural beauty, culture, and history.
The country is most famous for the Himalayas, which include Mount Everest, the highest peak in the world.
Nepal has a diverse landscape, ranging from snow-capped mountains in the north to fertile plains in the south.
The capital city, Kathmandu, is known for its historic temples, palaces, and vibrant cultural heritage.
Lumbini, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the birthplace of Lord Gautama Buddha, making Nepal an important destination for spiritual tourism.
The population is culturally diverse, with over 120 ethnic groups and numerous languages spoken across the country.
Major festivals include Dashain, Tihar, Holi, Teej, and Buddha Jayanti, reflecti ng the rich traditions and religious harmony.
Traditional clothing includes Daura Suruwal for men and Gunyo Cholo or Sari for women.
Agriculture and tourism are key parts of the economy, with trekkin g, mountaineering, and wildlife safaris attracting visitors from all over the world.
